1. What was the real root cause of the hospital pharmacy delays?

Understaffing during peak hours
Inexperienced pharmacists taking too long
Incomplete discharge orders creating rework loops
Outdated pharmacy computer systems
Correct! Lisa discovered that 40% of discharge medication orders contained errors or missing information requiring pharmacist clarification with physicians, creating rework loops rather than a simple staffing problem.

2. What is the key difference between symptoms and root causes?

Symptoms are harder to measure than root causes
Symptoms are observable effects; root causes are fundamental systemic issues
Symptoms always have single causes; root causes are complex
Symptoms occur first; root causes develop later
Right! Symptoms are the observable effects we can see (long cycle times, errors, complaints), while root causes are the fundamental systemic issues that create those problems.

Ask exactly 5 questions and stop

Perfect! Each "why" should be supported with evidence and drill deeper into the underlying cause, not just accept surface explanations or blame individuals.

6. Name two types of costs that should be included in a business case analysis.

Good answer! Business cases should include Direct Costs (labor, materials, rework) and Indirect Costs (customer satisfaction, employee turnover, opportunity costs, brand reputation impacts).

7. What is the primary purpose of Pareto analysis in root cause analysis?

To create visual diagrams of problems
To assign responsibility for problems
To identify the vital few causes contributing to most problems
To measure correlation between variables
Right! Pareto analysis helps identify the vital few causes that contribute to most of the problem (80/20 rule), allowing you to focus improvement efforts on the highest impact areas.

8. What are the four main data source types for process analysis?

Excellent! The four main data sources are: System Data (electronic records), Observational Data (direct measurement), Survey Data (stakeholder perceptions), and Interview Data (deep insights).

9. When designing a data collection strategy, what is most important for ensuring reliable conclusions?

Collecting as much data as possible
Representative sampling with adequate sample size
Using only quantitative data sources
Focusing on recent data only
Correct! Representative sampling that captures process variation with statistically adequate sample size is essential for reliable conclusions, regardless of total data volume.

10. What was the pattern Lisa discovered about physician response times during the week?

Consistent response times throughout the week
Faster responses on Mondays and Fridays
Slower responses during morning hours
3.2-hour average responses on Thursdays/Fridays due to surgery schedules
Perfect! Lisa found that physician response times averaged 3.2 hours on Thursdays and Fridays because doctors were in surgery or had heavy clinic schedules.

11. In hypothesis testing for process improvement, what should the null hypothesis state?

Process performance is not affected by proposed change
The proposed change will definitely improve performance
Current process performance is acceptable
The problem has multiple root causes
Excellent! The null hypothesis should state that the proposed change will have no effect, allowing you to test whether observed improvements are statistically significant.

12. What distinguishes control charts from other statistical tools in process analysis?

They only work with large sample sizes
They measure correlation between variables
They distinguish normal variation from special causes
They identify root causes automatically
Right! Control charts help distinguish between normal process variation (common causes) and unusual events (special causes) that require investigation.
