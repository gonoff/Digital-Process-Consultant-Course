Agile Governance Quiz

Instructions: Answer all questions to test your understanding of agile governance, Scrum-ban, and OKR frameworks. You need 80% (8/10) to pass.

Question 1: Scrum-ban Definition

What is the primary advantage of using Scrum-ban over traditional Scrum for business process improvement?

  • It eliminates the need for daily standups
  • It combines structure with continuous flow management
  • It requires fewer team members
  • It focuses only on software development
Correct Answer: It combines structure with continuous flow management. Scrum-ban provides sprint structure while allowing Kanban-style flow, perfect for operational improvements.

Question 2: OKR Components

In the OKR framework, what should be the ideal success rate for Key Results?

  • 100% - all Key Results should be achieved
  • 90% - nearly perfect execution
  • 70% - challenging but achievable
  • 50% - extremely ambitious targets
Correct Answer: 70% - challenging but achievable. This indicates the right level of ambition without being impossible.

Question 3: Decision Rights

According to the agile governance model, which type of decisions should teams be empowered to make?

  • All decisions affecting their work
  • Process changes affecting only their team
  • Strategic direction for the company
  • Budget allocation decisions
Correct Answer: Process changes affecting only their team. Teams should have autonomy within clear boundaries.

Question 4: Sprint Planning Scenario

Pacific Medical Services wants to reduce claim processing time. Their current average is 4.2 days, and they want to reach 2.8 days. The team has identified 8 potential improvements but can only work on 3 during the next 2-week sprint.

What should be the PRIMARY factor in selecting which 3 improvements to tackle first?

  • Easiest to implement
  • Highest impact on the sprint goal
  • Most technically interesting
  • Requested by management
Correct Answer: Highest impact on the sprint goal. Sprint planning should prioritize work that best contributes to achieving the sprint objective.

Question 5: Work-in-Progress Limits

What is the main purpose of setting Work-in-Progress (WIP) limits in a Kanban board?

  • To reduce team workload
  • To prevent overcommitment and improve flow
  • To eliminate the need for planning
  • To create more work for the team
Correct Answer: To prevent overcommitment and improve flow. WIP limits help teams focus and identify bottlenecks.

Question 8: Retrospective Focus

In a sprint retrospective, what should be the primary focus?

  • Blaming individuals for mistakes
  • Celebrating completed work
  • Improving team processes and collaboration
  • Planning the next sprint's work
Correct Answer: Improving team processes and collaboration. Retrospectives focus on how the team can work better together.

Question 9: Agile Governance Balance

Agile governance balances empowerment with accountability through:

  • Detailed approval processes
  • Transparency and clear boundaries
  • Micromanagement and control
  • Elimination of all oversight
Correct Answer: Transparency and clear boundaries. This allows autonomy while maintaining organizational alignment.

Question 10: Implementation Challenge

A manufacturing company implementing Scrum-ban finds that their production managers are reluctant to give teams decision-making authority. They continue to require approval for all process changes, creating delays and frustration.

What is the BEST approach to address this challenge?

  • Force immediate change to full empowerment
  • Start with clear decision boundaries and build trust gradually
  • Replace resistant managers
  • Abandon agile governance approach
Correct Answer: Start with clear decision boundaries and build trust gradually. This allows cultural change while maintaining stability.
