Level 3.1: Digital Transformation Frameworks Quiz

Question 1: MIT CISR Business Model Selection

TechFlow Solutions, a 200-employee software company, builds HR management tools that integrate with multiple payroll systems. They have detailed knowledge of their direct customers (HR departments) but limited visibility into end-users (employees). Which MIT CISR digital business model best fits their current position?

  • Supplier - They focus on operational excellence
  • Omnichannel - They have complete customer knowledge
  • Modular Producer - They create plug-and-play ecosystem products
  • Ecosystem Driver - They orchestrate multi-party value creation
Correct Answer: C
TechFlow Solutions fits the Modular Producer model because they operate in an ecosystem (integrating with multiple payroll systems) but have partial customer knowledge (know HR departments but not end-users). Their plug-and-play HR tools are designed to work within partner ecosystems, which is the hallmark of a Modular Producer.

Question 2: McKinsey 7-S Assessment Priority

A regional bank attempting digital transformation finds that despite investing in new technology (Systems), their initiatives keep failing. Staff survey reveals: "We have the tools, but nobody uses them properly. Different departments work in silos, and leadership still makes all decisions top-down." Which 7-S element should be addressed FIRST?

  • Skills - Train staff on the new technology
  • Structure - Address siloed departments and decision-making
  • Strategy - Develop a clearer digital vision
  • Systems - Upgrade to better technology
Correct Answer: B
The Structure element should be addressed first. The survey reveals organizational silos and centralized decision-making are blocking technology adoption. Without fixing the structural issues that prevent collaboration and empowerment, training (Skills) or better technology (Systems) won't solve the problem. Structure changes enable other improvements.

Question 4: Gartner Dual-Track Resource Allocation

A traditional manufacturing company operates in a stable industry with minimal digital disruption. They have $1M annual budget for digital initiatives. According to Gartner's framework, how should they allocate resources between optimization and transformation?

  • 80% transformation ($800K), 20% optimization ($200K)
  • 50% transformation ($500K), 50% optimization ($500K)
  • 20% transformation ($200K), 80% optimization ($800K)
  • 100% optimization ($1M), 0% transformation
Correct Answer: C
For companies in stable industries with low disruption, Gartner recommends allocating 80% to optimization and 20% to transformation. This allows them to improve efficiency and modernize existing processes while still exploring some transformative initiatives. Complete avoidance of transformation (Option D) leaves them vulnerable to future disruption.

Question 5: Digital Maturity Progression

Current State: Regional insurance broker with paper applications, phone-based customer service, Excel-based tracking, and email communication between agents.

3-Year Target: Digital-first operations with integrated systems and data-driven decision making.

What is the most realistic maturity level progression for this company?

  • Digital Resistant → Digital Disruptor
  • Digital Resistant → Digital Player
  • Digital Explorer → Digital Transformer
  • Digital Player → Digital Disruptor
Correct Answer: B
The company is currently Digital Resistant (paper-based, minimal technology). Reaching Digital Player (digital-first, integrated systems, data-driven) in 3 years is ambitious but realistic. Jumping to Disruptor (platform models, industry leadership) is unrealistic for a traditional broker. The progression must be gradual and sustainable.

Question 6: 7-S Interdependency Analysis

A healthcare network implements a new patient portal (Systems) but adoption is only 12%. Investigation reveals: doctors weren't consulted (Style), nurses lack digital skills (Skills), and the portal doesn't align with the mission of "personal patient care" (Shared Values). This scenario best illustrates which principle?

  • Hard elements are more important than soft elements
  • Technology implementation should always come first
  • All seven elements must be aligned for successful change
  • Shared values can be ignored in digital transformation
Correct Answer: C
This scenario perfectly illustrates that all seven elements must be aligned for successful change. The technology (Systems) failed because it wasn't supported by leadership approach (Style), staff capabilities (Skills), or organizational culture (Shared Values). McKinsey 7-S emphasizes that changing one element requires adjusting others for balance.

Question 7: Short Answer - ROI Calculation

A specialty retailer plans digital transformation with these projections:
- Year 1: $500K investment, $200K returns
- Year 2: $300K investment, $600K returns
- Year 3: $100K investment, $800K returns
Calculate the 3-year ROI percentage.

Sample Answer:
Total Investment: $500K + $300K + $100K = $900K
Total Returns: $200K + $600K + $800K = $1,600K
Net Benefit: $1,600K - $900K = $700K
ROI = (Net Benefit / Total Investment) × 100
ROI = ($700K / $900K) × 100 = 77.8%

Accept answers between 75-80% with reasonable calculation shown.

Question 8: Transformation vs. Optimization Decision

Which initiative represents digital TRANSFORMATION rather than optimization?

  • Moving from paper invoices to digital PDF invoices
  • Implementing automated email responses for common questions
  • Launching a subscription-based service model instead of one-time sales
  • Upgrading from on-premise servers to cloud hosting
Correct Answer: C
Launching a subscription model represents transformation because it fundamentally changes the business model and how value is delivered to customers. The other options (digital invoices, automated emails, cloud migration) are optimizations that improve existing processes without changing the core business model or value proposition.

Question 9: Framework Application Scenario

Company: 75-person accounting firm losing clients to automated tax software
Current Model: Traditional hourly billing for tax preparation
Resources: $2M available for 3-year transformation
Industry: Highly disrupted by TurboTax, QuickBooks, etc.

Which strategic approach is most appropriate?

  • Focus 80% on optimizing current tax prep processes
  • Develop new digital advisory services while maintaining tax prep
  • Completely abandon tax prep for consulting only
  • Wait for the disruption to pass before investing
Correct Answer: B
In a highly disrupted industry, the firm needs significant transformation (developing new digital services) while optimizing core business. This dual-track approach follows Gartner's framework for disrupted industries (40% optimization, 60% transformation). Complete abandonment is too risky, while focusing only on optimization ignores the existential threat.

Question 10: Digital Roadmap Sequencing

For a Digital Explorer-stage company, which transformation sequence is most logical?

  • Platform development → Data analytics → Process digitization → Skills training
  • Skills training → Process digitization → Data analytics → Platform development
  • Data analytics → Platform development → Skills training → Process digitization
  • Process digitization → Platform development → Data analytics → Skills training
Correct Answer: B
The logical sequence starts with skills training (building digital capabilities), then process digitization (creating digital workflows), followed by data analytics (leveraging digital data), and finally platform development (most advanced). This progression builds foundational capabilities before attempting complex transformations, matching the learning curve and resource requirements.
