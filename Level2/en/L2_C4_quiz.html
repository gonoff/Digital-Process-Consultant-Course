Question 1: AI Cost Calculation

Your AI system processes 1,000 customer emails monthly. Each email uses approximately 1,500 tokens for analysis and 800 tokens for response generation. At $0.03 per 1,000 tokens, what's the monthly AI cost?

  • $23 per month
  • $69 per month
  • $45 per month
  • $138 per month
Correct Answer: B
Total tokens per email: 1,500 + 800 = 2,300 tokens
Monthly tokens: 1,000 emails √ó 2,300 tokens = 2,300,000 tokens
Cost: 2,300,000 √∑ 1,000 √ó $0.03 = $69/month
Understanding token consumption is crucial for accurate AI project budgeting and ROI calculations.

Question 2: Prompt Engineering Best Practices

Which prompt structure element is most critical for ensuring consistent, business-appropriate AI outputs?

  • Using the highest possible temperature setting for creativity
  • Role definition with specific business context and constraints
  • Requesting the longest possible response for thoroughness
  • Avoiding any examples to prevent bias
Correct Answer: B
Role definition with business context provides the AI with necessary constraints and expectations for appropriate responses. This includes company policies, tone requirements, and output standards. High temperature increases randomness (bad for consistency), response length doesn't ensure quality, and examples actually help guide appropriate responses.

Question 4: RAG System Architecture

In a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system for customer support, what happens when the knowledge base doesn't contain relevant information for a query?

  • The AI should make up a plausible answer based on general knowledge
  • The system should gracefully indicate insufficient information and escalate to human agents
  • The AI should provide the closest match even if it's not relevant
  • The system should automatically search the internet for answers
Correct Answer: B
RAG systems should gracefully handle knowledge gaps by explicitly stating when information is insufficient and routing to human experts. This prevents hallucination (making up information) and maintains trust. Providing irrelevant matches or general internet searches can lead to inaccurate business-specific advice.

Question 5: Human Oversight Framework

For a legal document review AI system, which factor should trigger mandatory human review?

  • Document length exceeds 5 pages
  • AI confidence score below 85% on risk assessment
  • Document was created on a weekend
  • Client is requesting expedited review
Correct Answer: B
Low confidence scores indicate the AI is uncertain about its analysis, requiring human expertise. Document length, creation timing, and expedited requests don't inherently indicate quality or risk issues. Confidence thresholds ensure human review when AI uncertainty is high, maintaining quality standards.

Question 6: AI Implementation Risk Assessment

Which represents the highest risk in AI-enhanced business processes?

  • Higher operational costs than expected
  • Staff resistance to new technology
  • AI hallucination leading to incorrect business decisions
  • Slower processing speed than manual methods
Correct Answer: C
AI hallucination (generating false but plausible information) poses the highest risk because it can lead to serious business consequences like legal liability, financial losses, or compliance violations. Cost overruns, staff resistance, and speed issues are manageable operational challenges, but incorrect information can cause irreversible damage.

Question 8: AI Integration Pattern Selection

A medical practice wants to automate insurance pre-authorization requests. Which AI integration pattern is most appropriate?

EMR Data ‚Üí AI Analysis ‚Üí Pre-auth Form ‚Üí Human Review ‚Üí Submission vs. EMR Data ‚Üí AI Analysis ‚Üí Direct Submission ‚Üí Post-processing Review vs. Manual Data Entry ‚Üí AI Assistance ‚Üí Human Decision ‚Üí Manual Submission
  • Direct submission pattern for maximum efficiency
  • Human review before submission pattern for safety and compliance
  • Manual assistance pattern to maintain human control
  • Fully automated pattern with no human involvement
Correct Answer: B
Healthcare applications require human review before submission due to patient safety, regulatory compliance, and liability concerns. Medical decisions and insurance submissions have legal implications requiring physician oversight. Direct submission risks errors with serious consequences, while manual assistance doesn't leverage AI efficiency benefits.

Question 9: Quality Assurance Metrics

For an AI customer service system, which metric combination best indicates system health and reliability?

  • Response time and token usage
  • Accuracy rate, human override frequency, and customer satisfaction
  • Daily transaction volume and system uptime
  • Cost per interaction and processing speed
Correct Answer: B
Accuracy rate measures AI performance quality, human override frequency indicates when AI falls short, and customer satisfaction measures business impact. This combination covers technical performance, human-AI interaction, and business outcomes. Response time, costs, and volume are operational metrics but don't indicate quality or reliability.

Question 10: Scaling AI Implementations

When scaling an AI-enhanced process from 100 to 5,000 monthly transactions, which consideration is most critical?

  • Maintaining the same prompt templates without modification
  • Implementing robust monitoring and quality control systems
  • Reducing human oversight to manage increased volume
  • Switching to a cheaper AI model to control costs
Correct Answer: B
At scale, robust monitoring becomes critical to detect quality degradation, identify patterns in failures, and maintain consistent performance. Small issues become major problems at high volume. Prompts may need refinement, human oversight remains important for quality, and cheaper models might compromise accuracy when business stakes are higher.
